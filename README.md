# AI Coding Agents Study

This repository contains the materials for a small study on the use of AI coding agents and their impact on work, productivity, cognitive effort, well-being, and perceived business outcomes.

The study was conducted to better understand how people experience working with AI coding agents in practice: what becomes easier, what benefits they perceive, what new demands appear, and how these tools may reshape the nature of work. Responses were collected anonymously and intended to be analyzed only in aggregate as part of an essay on the implications of AI coding agents in contemporary work.

The survey itself was designed to take around 1-2 minutes to complete and included 15 questions covering areas such as:

- execution speed and productivity
- supervision and review effort
- control and technical confidence
- business outcomes
- mental exhaustion and work-related stress
- satisfaction, recovery, and creative time

The dataset in this folder contains **89 anonymous responses**, and the accompanying essay draft develops the interpretation of those findings.
